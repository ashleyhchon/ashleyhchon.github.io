Lunar Impact

MATLAB  |  Apr - May 2020

‍

The goal of this design project was to design the trajectory of a satellite that would be launched to impact the moon. The MATLAB program was written to calculate the orbits of the satellite and the moon and used to calculate critical mission parameters, such as dates that the impact can take place, the position of the impact; the trajectory of the satellite necessary to hit the moon, and the impact velocity.

To summarize the results of the design project, the two points where the lunar impact may take place are at (-347463, -83243.7, -4268.91) and (394949, 94620.1, 4852.32) in kilometers. The time for the moon to reach the first target point was 0.739731 days and 14.5458 days for the second target point. The speed to hit the descending impact point was 0.675 km/s and took 288590 seconds. The speed to hit the ascending impact point was 1 km/s and took 29277.1 seconds. The impact velocity with the moon was found to be 2.53482 km/s.
